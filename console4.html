Standard Procedure Framework

1. Initial Report (Intake)

Report received from public, FB, or stakeholders. Mandatory reporting obligation triggers.

2. Initial Assessment & Triage

Immediate risk assessment. Urgent cases escalated within 24 hours.

3. Investigation & Evidence Collection

Gather evidence (documents, videos, voice notes, testimonies). Chain of custody maintained.

4. Multi-Stakeholder Referral

Assign duty bearers (health, police, teachers). Track referral chain.

5. Intervention & Action Tracking

Monitor actions by each stakeholder. Log all communications.

6. Legal/Court Process (if needed)

Escalate to courts for mandatory cases. Follow Children's Act procedures.

7. Monitoring & Follow-Up

Ongoing support and case review. Ensure outcomes achieved.

8. Case Closure & Documentation

Final assessment. All documentation complete. Archive case.

Kenyan Legal Framework

Children Act 2022 (Kenya): Primary legislation protecting children's rights and welfare. All procedures must comply.

Data Protection Act 2019: ODPC compliance for handling personal information. Mandatory reporting without consent for child protection.

Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act 2018: Guidelines for digital evidence collection and privacy in digital investigations.

National Referral Pathway: Protocol for referrals across institutions and duty bearers.

Victim Support Act 2014: Protection and support services for victims, especially children.

Mandatory Report Cases

• Child abuse, neglect, or exploitation

• Abandonment or destitution

• Trafficking for any purpose

• Sexual violence or exploitation

• Harmful traditional practices (FGM, child marriage)

• Child labor violations

• Denial of education or health services

⚠️ Failure to report these cases is a legal violation under Children Act 2022
